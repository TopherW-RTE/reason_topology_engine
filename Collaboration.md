why an NDA exists, how to initiate contact, and what happens after you do.

## What's Public vs. What's Not

**This repository contains:**

- The complete architecture blueprint — pipeline, topology schema, ledger,
  scaffold injection design
- The empirical validation findings from 1,530 pipeline runs across three
  temperature settings

**Not publicly disclosed at this time:**
- The calibration methodology for the six-gate scoring framework
- The bidirectional topology internals — how human reasoning traces are
  extracted, scored, and fused with model traces
- The mitotic cascade design — the self-organizing branching and pruning
  logic for the topology ledger
- The gate weight derivations and the thermodynamic foundations of each gate
- The empirical dataset (1,530 runs) in raw form
- Active development work on the generative gate validator

The public repository is a complete, working system and a strong signal
of the research direction. The work listed above is what the NDA creates
space to discuss.

## Why an NDA?

The system contains novel architectural IP that has not yet been fully
disclosed in public research. Some components are in active development.
Others are intentionally withheld pending publication of subsequent work.

The NDA is mutual. It protects both parties. You are not being asked to
sign something one-sided — the same confidentiality obligations apply in
both directions. This means you can speak freely about your own work and
interests during any collaboration conversation without concern that
information will leave the room.

The NDA is not a wall. It is a container that makes genuine technical
conversation possible. It exists so that both parties can engage with the
actual work rather than a sanitized version of it.

## What Collaboration Can Look Like

The following are possibilities, not promises. The appropriate form of
engagement depends on your background and interest.

- **Technical deep-dive** — walking through the bidirectional topology
  architecture, the gate derivations, and the calibration methodology in
  detail
- **Calibration collaboration** — working with the empirical dataset to
  develop or validate the generative gate validator
- **Research partnership** — co-authoring subsequent arXiv papers or
  contributing to the formal specification of the bidirectional extension
- **Integration exploration** — examining how this architecture could
  interface with existing reasoning, evaluation, or agent frameworks
- **Institutional or funding partnership** — exploring what a more
  resourced development environment would look like for this research

If your interest does not fit neatly into any of these categories, reach
out anyway. The conversation can define its own shape.

## What Happens After Signing

The initial conversation is not a pitch or a gatekeeping exercise. It is
a genuine exchange. Expect to cover your background and what drew you to
this work, which aspects of the system are most relevant to your interest,
and what a productive engagement might look like for both parties.

Format is flexible — video call or async text, whichever works better.
Duration is typically 30 to 60 minutes for an initial conversation.

Disclosure is progressive. Signing the NDA opens the door to the
conversation. Deeper technical detail is shared as the collaboration
takes shape and mutual fit becomes clear. Nobody receives full access
on day one and nobody is asked to commit to anything in the first
conversation.

## FAQ

**Can I use the public code without signing anything?**
Yes. The code in this repository is released under the Apache 2.0 license.
You can use it, modify it, and build on it freely within those terms.
No NDA is required to run the system, experiment with it, or integrate
it into your own work. The NDA is only relevant if you want to engage
with the non-public research described above.

**How long does the NDA last?**
The duration is specified in the document. Standard term is two to three
years from the date of signing, with specific provisions detailed in the
agreement itself.

**Do I need a lawyer before signing?**
Not for the initial conversation. The NDA is written in plain language
and is intended to be readable without legal expertise. If you are
representing an institution or considering a formal partnership, your
organization's standard counsel review process applies — that is
appropriate and expected.

**What if I have a question about the NDA terms before signing?**
Reach out via email before signing. Questions about specific provisions
are welcome and will be answered directly.

**Is this research affiliated with any institution?**
No. This is independent research. All work documented here was conducted
and funded independently.
